Diseases in hens can be caused by a variety of factors, including viruses, bacteria, parasites, and environmental stress. Common chicken diseases include avian influenza, salmonellosis, fowlpox, and Newcastle disease. Symptoms can range from respiratory issues and diarrhea to decreased egg production and death. Preventing and controlling the spread of diseases in poultry farms is crucial for both animal welfare and food safety.
Instant alert for the farmer
Poultry alert refers to a situation where a disease outbreak or threat of disease is identified in a poultry population, requiring immediate action to prevent its spread. This can include measures such as culling infected birds, implementing biosecurity measures, and monitoring and testing the surrounding poultry populations. Poultry alerts are taken seriously by governments, the poultry industry, and the general public due to the potential economic and public health impacts of a disease outbreak.

Livestock health at your fingertips refers to the use of technology such as mobile apps, websites, and software that provide farmers and livestock owners with quick and easy access to information about animal health and disease management.
